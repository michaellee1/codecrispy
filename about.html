About

I started programming in the 7th grade, only inspired to start coding by my brother, who majored in computer science. All throughout elementary and middle school, I had yet to see one class on computer programming, an essential skill that powers all of our technology. The very first opportunity came in high school, which came with two Java classes. Although better than most high schools can muster, it is a classic "too little too late."

Even though I learned so comparatively early, I only really got into it one and a half years after I had begun typing out my first lines of code. After finding an inner interest, a “spark”, and asking a few fellow high school coders I met at MIT about their 'spark', I realized that everyone who is a “programmer” had a certain revelation of sorts that really pushed them to where they are now. It is interest, and not experience, that creates this spark.

For me and for many other coders, this spark was created when I finally realized what coding meant. It wasn’t about knowing the syntax for lambda expressions or an AJAX call, it was finally realizing that your imagination could now be passed to the computer.

Code Crispy’s goal is to pass that same inspiration onto students who want to give programming a try. It does this through educating younger children, and through targeted donations.

Our curriculum has a simple philosophy - Theory over syntax. I believe its more important for kids to understand what they can implement than exactly how its done. This means that younger elementary school children will see the theory behind more advanced notions, like scalable servers, multithreading, database pulls, and other great amazing things that can hook you in and really help to get kids thinking about what they can pull off. The syntax is always there to guide them and really get them used to reading in code, but its clearly far more important to be thinking ‘It is possible, and I know the theory’ versus ‘It isn’t possible for me.’

The money that we collect will be donated to other charities that have these values and ideals, in order to further our cause in a way we can’t do ourselves. If you like our cause and want to help us out, the best way to do so is through a donation. Your money will go toward helping kids find their next opportunity.
